## **SECTION 1 · GETTING TO KNOW US** 

## **Our Story** 

NovaSprint Labs was founded in 2021 by three friends who were frustrated with clunky, overcomplicated project management tools. They believed small teams deserved software that was simple, fast, and actually enjoyable to use. 

That idea became SprintBoard, our flagship product — a lightweight project and task management tool built for startups and small teams. SprintBoard is now used by thousands of teams around the world to plan sprints, track tasks, and ship work faster. 

What started as three people working out of a small shared office has grown into a team of around 25 people across engineering, design, operations, and customer support. We are still small enough that everyone knows everyone, and that is something we want to protect as we grow. 

Today, NovaSprint Labs is a remote-friendly company with a home base office that the team uses for collaboration days and events. We are profitable, growing steadily, and focused on building a product our customers genuinely love. 

Our journey is just getting started, and you are now a part of it.

NovaSprint Labs has around 25 employees organized into four departments: Engineering, Design, Operations, and Customer Support. Each department has a lead who reports directly to the Co-Founders. 

We intentionally keep our structure flat. There are no layers of middle management between an individual contributor and a decision-maker. This means your ideas can reach leadership quickly, and it also means everyone is expected to communicate clearly and proactively. 

Departments at NovaSprint Labs work closely together rather than in silos. Engineers sit in on design reviews, support specialists share customer feedback directly with product teams, and operations supports every department behind the scenes.

## **SECTION 3 · OUR TEAM** 

## **Engineering Department** 

The Engineering department is the largest team at NovaSprint Labs, with around 10 people. Engineering builds, tests, and maintains SprintBoard and the systems behind it. 

The department is organized into three informal groups: Frontend (the SprintBoard interface), Backend (servers, databases, and APIs), and Quality Assurance (testing and release quality). Many engineers work across more than one area.

## **SECTION 3 · OUR TEAM** 

## **How We Work Together** 

Every employee at NovaSprint Labs reports to a department lead, who in turn reports to one of the Co-Founders. This keeps reporting lines short and clear. 

We use a simple weekly rhythm to stay aligned: each department holds a Monday planning meeting, a company-wide update is shared every Friday afternoon, and one-on-one meetings between employees and managers happen at least once every two weeks.

## **Attendance Policy**

## **SECTION 5 · WORKING AT NOVASPRINT** 

## **Communication Guidelines** 

Clear communication is essential for a small, remote-friendly team like ours. These guidelines help us stay aligned without drowning in unnecessary meetings or messages. 

Default to written communication for anything that needs a record, such as decisions, requests, or instructions. Use calls or meetings for discussions that benefit from real-time conversation. 

Keep messages clear and specific. Instead of “Can we talk?”, try “Can we talk about the delay on the export feature? I have a quick question about scope.” This helps the other person prepare and respond faster. 

Respond to direct Slack messages within one business day, even if it is just to say you will follow up later. Silence creates uncertainty on a small team. 

Assume good intent. Text-based communication can sometimes sound blunter than intended; when in doubt, ask for clarification before assuming the worst.

**SECTION 5 · WORKING AT NOVASPRINT** 

## **The Review Cycle** 

NovaSprint Labs runs a formal performance review twice a year, in June and December. This is in addition to the regular one-on-one feedback employees receive throughout the year. 

## **How the Review Works** 

- Self-assessment: you reflect on your goals, wins, and challenges from the period. 

- Peer feedback: one or two colleagues you work closely with share input. 

- Manager review: your manager combines this input with their own observations. 

- Review conversation: you and your manager discuss the review together and set goals for the next period. 

Reviews at NovaSprint Labs do not use numeric scores or forced rankings. The focus is on clear, constructive conversation about growth, strengths, and areas to develop. 

Compensation conversations, where relevant, happen separately from the review conversation so that growth feedback and pay discussions do not get mixed together.

## **SECTION 6 · SAFETY AND SECURITY** 

## **Data Security Practices** 

Good security habits protect both the company and our customers. These practices are simple but make a real difference. 

- Use two-factor authentication (2FA) on all company accounts, including email, Slack, and the code repository. 

- Use a unique, strong password for each work account, ideally managed through a password manager. 

- Lock your laptop screen whenever you step away, even at home. 

- Avoid using public Wi-Fi for work without a VPN; use your phone's hotspot if needed. 

- Never share your login credentials with anyone, including colleagues. 

If you ever suspect a security issue, such as a phishing email, a lost device, or unusual account activity, report it immediately to the #it-support Slack channel. Acting quickly is more important than figuring out exactly what happened first.

## **SECTION 7 · REWARDS AND MILESTONES** 

## **Exit Process** 

Whether you are moving on to a new opportunity or another life change, we want your departure from NovaSprint Labs to be smooth and respectful, just like your time here. 

Employees are asked to provide at least 4 weeks' written notice before their last working day, by informing their manager and People Operations directly.
